The research which was presented in this pre-thesis 1 report aims to develop a multi-task deep learning framework for unified cattle health and behavior monitoring using public RGB and depth datasets. We are hopeful that this research will contribute significantly to precision livestock farming. Working with diverse cattle health monitoring tasks is highly challenging due to the complexity of integrating multiple objectives and the limited processing capacity of edge devices. Our framework strives to robustly monitor and protect cattle health indicators from potential adversarial scenarios and corrupted data. Our research does not cover everything; there is huge ground to cover. Agricultural sectors are rapidly evolving and slowly blurring the line between traditional and smart farming. Collaborative and federated learning approaches can serve as great solutions for cattle health monitoring. So, protecting these models from attacks and data corruption should be considered a top priority. The integration of RGB and depth modalities, combined with MTL, provides us with a strong approach in the way of automating cattle health assessments that improve animal welfare and productivity. In the future, we will focus on the deployment of these models in real-world environments to validate performance across different cattle breeds and conditions, and explore federated learning for secure collaborative training without centralizing data while preserving data privacy.
